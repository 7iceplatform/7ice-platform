# Service Management

## Purpose

Service management handles post-delivery requests, incidents, planned maintenance, warranties, and remediation. It turns a customer issue into an owned, measurable case and, where needed, a dispatched work order.

## Case lifecycle

Cases are classified by type, severity, entitlement, asset/configuration, location, and customer impact. They move through New, Triaged, Awaiting Customer, Planned, In Progress, Resolved, Confirmed, and Closed states. Reopening is allowed only under defined policy and preserves the full chronology. Service-level clocks pause only for documented, policy-approved reasons.

## Operating model

- Triage verifies identity, safety risk, entitlement, duplication, and urgency before assigning an owner.
- Knowledge-guided remote resolution is preferred where safe; on-site work creates or links to a work order.
- Parts, third-party visits, and customer appointments are tracked as dependent commitments, not free-text notes.
- Resolution records cause, action, customer-facing summary, evidence, and eligibility for billing or warranty.

High-risk issues follow incident escalation and customer communication policies.
