# Product Principles

1. **Lifecycle over silos.** Design around the customer and operational journey, with clear ownership at each handoff.
2. **Truth is attributable.** Status, commitments, and financial events show their source, time, and responsible actor or system.
3. **Progressive complexity.** Reveal advanced control when users need it; do not reduce essential accuracy to make a screen look simple.
4. **Configuration with guardrails.** Tenants can adapt content, policy, and approved presentation without undermining security, accessibility, or shared domain truth.
5. **Humans stay in charge.** Automation proposes, routes, and accelerates; high-impact decisions remain explainable, controllable, and auditable.
6. **Design for exceptions.** Incomplete readiness, disputed data, failed delivery, and unavailable dependencies receive first-class recovery paths.
7. **Privacy earns trust.** Request the minimum data, explain purpose, honor preferences, and protect sensitive context by default.
8. **Evidence before optimism.** Measure outcomes in production and revise assumptions when user behavior or operations contradict them.
